## Claim Statement

Since m = c times chi, engineering local chi could produce effective mass engineering.

## Assumptions

- The relation `m = c * chi` remains valid under controlled local perturbations of `chi`.
- Local `chi` can be modified independently from trivial thermal or mechanical artifacts.
- Effective mass change can be measured with calibrated inertial and gravitational probes.

## Mathematical Form

- `m_eff(x) = c * chi(x)`
- `Delta m_eff = c * Delta chi`

## Potential Falsifier

- If controlled `Delta chi` does not produce measurable and repeatable `Delta m_eff` above instrument/systematic bounds, the claim is falsified.

## Evidence / Notes

- Claim is explicitly speculative and requires strict control-energy accounting before physical interpretation.
