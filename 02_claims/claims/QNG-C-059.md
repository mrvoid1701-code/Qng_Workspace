## Claim Statement

Post-merger galaxy clusters should retain observable memory signatures in gravitational lensing maps due to delayed relaxation of the stability field, resulting in lensing structures that reflect historical mass configurations rather than instantaneous baryonic distributions alone.

## Assumptions

- A1. The stability field Σ\SigmaΣ evolves with finite relaxation time and memory.
- A2. Cluster mergers create rapidly changing mass–Straton distributions.
- A3. Stability-field adaptation lags behind instantaneous mass redistribution.
- A4. Lensing traces gradients of the stability field rather than baryonic mass alone.
- A5. Memory effects persist over timescales comparable to relaxation time.

- Observations showing lensing maps in post-merger clusters consistent solely with instantaneous baryonic distributions.
- Measurements incompatible with any delayed or historical gravitational effects.
- Demonstration that lensing signatures evolve instantaneously after mergers.
- Empirical necessity for particle dark matter explanations independent of memory dynamics.
