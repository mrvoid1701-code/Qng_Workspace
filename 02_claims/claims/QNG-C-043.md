## Claim Statement

High-mass systems correspond to node configurations exhibiting stronger persistence under intrinsic fluctuations, reflecting increased resistance to dynamical perturbations within the update network.

## Assumptions

- A1. Mass is related to temporal stability and persistence of node configurations.
- A2. Fluctuations continuously perturb node states during evolution.
- A3. Configurations with higher stability are more resistant to perturbations.
- A4. Macroscopic mass emerges from aggregated persistence of constituent nodes.
- A5. Stability-driven persistence determines the robustness of physical structures.

- Empirical necessity for alternative mechanisms determining mass robustness.
