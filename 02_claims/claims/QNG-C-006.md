## Claim Statement

Absolute space, time, and force do not exist as independent fundamental entities; they arise only as emergent descriptions of the collective behavior and interactions of discrete informational nodes within the relational network.

## Assumptions

- A1. All physical quantities derive from node states and their relational interactions.
- A2. No external background manifold or coordinate system exists independently of node connectivity.
- A3. Temporal ordering emerges from update dynamics rather than from an independent temporal dimension.
- A4. Apparent forces correspond to effective responses to stability gradients and relational tensions within the network.
- A5. Observable physical laws emerge through coarse-graining of node interactions.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental detection of an absolute spatial or temporal background independent of physical processes.
- Evidence requiring a preferred universal reference frame incompatible with relational emergence.
- Observations demonstrating forces that cannot be represented as emergent responses from underlying dynamics.
- Empirical necessity of external spacetime structure independent of matter or interactions.
