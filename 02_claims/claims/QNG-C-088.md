## Claim Statement

Galaxies with older dynamical histories and limited recent mass inflow may exhibit gradual dark-halo weakening or decay as stability-field memory relaxes toward equilibrium over time.

## Assumptions

- A1. Dark-halo–like effects arise from stability-memory rather than permanent unseen mass.
- A2. Stability memory evolves with finite relaxation timescale.
- A3. Older systems have experienced longer relaxation without significant new informational input.
- A4. Observable halo strength reflects the current state of stability-field relaxation.
- A5. Isolation conditions accelerate memory decay relative to actively evolving systems.

## Potential Falsifier

- Observations showing halo strength independent of galaxy age or dynamical history.
- Evidence requiring persistent unseen mass regardless of relaxation processes.
- Demonstration that halo decay cannot occur under realistic conditions.
- Empirical necessity for particle dark matter independent of memory effects.
