## Claim Statement

At the fundamental level, there exists no continuous spatial interpolation between nodes; spatial continuity arises only as an emergent approximation from densely connected node ensembles.

## Assumptions

- A1. Nodes constitute irreducible spatial units with no internal continuous substructure.
- A2. Physical adjacency is defined exclusively by relational connections in the node graph.
- A3. Macroscopic spatial continuity emerges through coarse-graining over large node populations.
- A4. Observable geometry is insensitive to discrete gaps at scales much larger than the node scale.
- A5. Interactions propagate through discrete relational links rather than through continuous media.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence of continuous spatial degrees of freedom at arbitrarily small scales.
- Observations requiring intermediate spatial states between fundamental units.
- Empirical confirmation that physical processes require continuous interpolation independent of relational structure.
- Detection of phenomena incompatible with any discrete spatial topology.
