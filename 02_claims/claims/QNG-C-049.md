## Claim Statement

Quantum phenomena such as superposition, tunneling, and decoherence arise from transitions between stability regimes within node dynamics, particularly near persistence thresholds where multiple configurations compete under fluctuations.

## Assumptions

- A1. Node configurations may occupy multiple quasi-stable states near the stability threshold.
- A2. Fluctuations enable transitions between competing configurations.
- A3. Phase coherence governs interference-like behavior among configurations.
- A4. Environmental interactions modify stability and drive transitions toward dominant configurations.
- A5. Classical behavior emerges when one configuration becomes strongly stable relative to others.
