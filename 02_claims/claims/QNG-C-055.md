## Claim Statement

The magnitude of directional gravitational anomalies produced by stratonic lag depends on the orientation of system velocity relative to spatial gradients of the stability field.

## Assumptions

- A1. Residual accelerations arise from delayed adaptation of the stability field.
- A2. Lag-induced effects depend on the velocity vector of the moving system.
- A3. Stability gradients vary spatially across the network.
- A4. The interaction between motion and gradients produces orientation-dependent effects.
- A5. Perturbative approximations are valid for small lag relative to system scales.
