## Claim Statement

In regimes beyond classical limits, observable phenomena should include stability-memory effects, lag-induced dynamical deviations, and phase decoherence behavior arising from underlying node dynamics and relaxation processes.

## Assumptions

- A1. Classical physics emerges when relaxation delay and fluctuations are negligible.
- A2. Non-classical regimes occur when memory, delay, or phase effects become significant.
- A3. Stability-field dynamics influence both gravitational and quantum behavior.
- A4. Phase coherence among nodes can degrade due to fluctuations and interactions.
- A5. Observable deviations reflect underlying discrete update processes.
