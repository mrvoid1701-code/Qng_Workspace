## Claim Statement

Stratonic lag is given by
ΔL=v τ,\Delta L = v \, \tau,ΔL=vτ,
where vvv is the characteristic velocity of mass–Straton transport and τ\tauτ is the relaxation delay associated with informational load, representing the spatial offset between instantaneous mass configuration and gravitational response.

## Assumptions

- A1. Systems with informational load exhibit finite temporal relaxation delay τ\tauτ.
- A2. Mass or Straton distributions may move with velocity vvv relative to the surrounding network.
- A3. Gravitational or stability-field response reflects delayed adaptation to changing configurations.
- A4. Spatial offsets arise from the combination of motion and relaxation delay.
- A5. Macroscopic lag phenomena emerge from aggregated node-level delays.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing no spatial offset between moving mass distributions and gravitational response.
- Observations incompatible with any velocity–delay proportional lag relation.
- Demonstration that gravitational or stability-field response is instantaneous under all conditions.
- Empirical data contradicting predicted lag magnitudes derived from vτv \tauvτ.

## Evidence / Notes

- Dimensionally consistent relation linking motion and temporal delay to spatial offset.
- Provides a mechanism for gravitational memory and lag phenomena.
- Conceptually analogous to delayed-response systems in physics.
- Empirical validation depends on detecting measurable offsets or timing anomalies.
