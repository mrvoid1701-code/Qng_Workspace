## Claim Statement

Probes with high informational load χ\chiχ undergoing directed motion through spatial stability gradients should exhibit measurable acceleration anomalies due to enhanced lag effects in the stability-field response.

## Assumptions

- A1. Informational load χ\chiχ modulates relaxation delay τ\tauτ.
- A2. Directed motion through stability gradients produces lag-induced residual accelerations.
- A3. Higher χ\chiχ corresponds to stronger resistance to state adaptation and larger effective delay.
- A4. Perturbative approximations are valid for small lag relative to system scales.
- A5. Observed anomalies arise from interaction between motion, stability gradients, and informational load.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing no acceleration anomalies correlated with informational load.
- Observations incompatible with lag-dependent acceleration scaling.
- Demonstration that probe motion cannot produce residual accelerations under QNG assumptions.
- Empirical necessity for alternative explanations independent of χ\chiχ.
