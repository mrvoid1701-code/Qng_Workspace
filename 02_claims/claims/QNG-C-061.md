## Claim Statement

Dark matter halo–like gravitational structures can gradually fade over time in old, dynamically isolated systems due to relaxation of the stability-field memory in the absence of significant new mass or informational inflow.

## Assumptions

- A1. Halo effects arise from stability-field memory rather than permanent unseen mass.
- A2. Stability-field memory evolves with finite relaxation timescale.
- A3. Isolated systems experience minimal new informational input.
- A4. Memory contributions decay toward equilibrium distributions over time.
- A5. Observable gravitational effects reflect the current state of stability-field relaxation.

## Potential Falsifier

- Observational evidence showing halo structures remain constant indefinitely in isolated systems.
- Measurements incompatible with any relaxation or decay of halo effects.
- Demonstration that gravitational halos require permanent unseen mass.
- Empirical necessity for particle dark matter independent of memory dynamics.
