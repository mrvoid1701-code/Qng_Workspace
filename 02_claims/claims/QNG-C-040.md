## Claim Statement

Dark matter halo–like gravitational structures are time-dependent manifestations of stability-field memory and may dissipate or relax over long timescales as the system approaches equilibrium.

## Assumptions

- A1. Gravitational behavior is governed by the stability field Σ\SigmaΣ with finite relaxation time.
- A2. Stability-field memory retains historical contributions from past mass–Straton configurations.
- A3. Systems evolve toward equilibrium distributions over long timescales.
- A4. Memory-induced gravitational enhancements diminish as relaxation progresses.
- A5. Observable halo structures correspond to transient or quasi-stationary states rather than permanent mass distributions.
