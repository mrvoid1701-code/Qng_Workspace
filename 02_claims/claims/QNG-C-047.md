## Claim Statement

Identity corresponds to a persistent relational pattern within the node network rather than a fixed absolute position in space, emerging from stable configurations of connections and state variables over time.

## Assumptions

- A1. Node configurations are defined by relational connections rather than absolute coordinates.
- A2. Persistence of relational structure determines identity over time.
- A3. Node positions may change while relational patterns remain invariant.
- A4. Observable objects correspond to clusters maintaining relational stability.
- A5. Macroscopic identity emerges from sustained persistence of network patterns.

## Potential Falsifier

- Observations incompatible with relational persistence as the basis of identity.
- Demonstration that relational patterns cannot account for object continuity.
- Empirical necessity for absolute-position–based identity definitions.
