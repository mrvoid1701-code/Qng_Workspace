## Claim Statement

Dark-halo profiles should differ between galaxies that have similar present-day visible mass distributions but distinct evolutionary histories, due to differences in accumulated stability-memory contributions.

## Assumptions

- A1. Stability field Σ\SigmaΣ retains historical information through memory kernels.
- A2. Galaxy formation and interaction histories influence the evolution of informational load χ\chiχ.
- A3. Effective gravitational profiles depend on both instantaneous mass and historical contributions.
- A4. Observed halo structure reflects accumulated memory rather than only current baryonic mass.
- A5. Systems with similar present mass but different histories can produce different halo signatures.

- Evidence that halo properties depend only on present baryonic mass.
- Demonstration that historical effects cannot influence gravitational profiles.
- Empirical necessity for particle dark matter independent of system history.
