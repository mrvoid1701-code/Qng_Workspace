## Claim Statement

The formal Composite Time Mechanics (CTM) formulation includes a fluctuation–coupling contribution from stability variations, such that observed time incorporates an additional term proportional to δΣ\delta \SigmaδΣ.

## Assumptions

- A1. Physical time corresponds to composite update dynamics rather than a single fundamental parameter.
- A2. Stability-field fluctuations influence temporal evolution of node configurations.
- A3. Observed temporal intervals reflect both deterministic and fluctuation-driven contributions.
- A4. Informational load and environmental interactions modulate fluctuation coupling.
- A5. Macroscopic timing behavior emerges from aggregated microscopic dynamics.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing no timing effects correlated with stability fluctuations.
- Observations incompatible with fluctuation-induced temporal variations.
- Demonstration that composite time cannot include fluctuation-dependent terms.
- Empirical necessity for purely deterministic timing models.

## Evidence / Notes

- Extends CTM to include stochastic contributions to temporal behavior.
- Conceptually consistent with fluctuation-driven dynamics in complex systems.
- Provides a mechanism for small deviations from deterministic time evolution.
- Empirical validation depends on detecting measurable fluctuation-related timing effects.
