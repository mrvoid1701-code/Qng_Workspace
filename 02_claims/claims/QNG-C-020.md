## Claim Statement

Observed time is a composite quantity consisting of reference update progression, intrinsic relaxation delay associated with informational load, and additional memory-coupled contributions arising from stability-field interactions.

## Assumptions

- A1. Fundamental dynamics proceed through discrete update steps defining reference progression.
- A2. Physical systems exhibit intrinsic relaxation delays dependent on informational load (Straton content).
- A3. Stability-field gradients introduce additional temporal modulation through memory coupling.
- A4. Observed temporal intervals emerge from the combination of these contributions rather than from a single fundamental parameter.
- A5. Macroscopic continuous time arises through coarse-graining of composite temporal processes.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence demonstrating that observed time intervals depend solely on a single fundamental parameter independent of relaxation or memory effects.
- Observations incompatible with any delay contributions linked to informational load or stability gradients.
- Demonstration that temporal behavior cannot be decomposed into multiple contributing mechanisms.
- Empirical necessity for strictly geometric time without dynamical corrections.
