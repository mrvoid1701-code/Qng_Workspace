## Claim Statement

Matter corresponds to stable attractor configurations within the node update network, representing persistent structures that remain invariant under local update dynamics and fluctuations.

## Assumptions

- A1. Node dynamics generate a range of configurations through local interactions and stochastic processes.
- A2. Stability determines the persistence probability of configurations.
- A3. Attractor states correspond to configurations resilient to perturbations.
- A4. Observable matter corresponds to clusters maintaining stability above a critical threshold.
- A5. Macroscopic properties emerge from aggregated node attractor structures.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing matter configurations that do not correspond to stable attractor states.
- Observations incompatible with persistence emerging from dynamical stability.
- Demonstration that node dynamics cannot produce stable configurations resembling matter.
- Empirical necessity for fundamentally different mechanisms of matter existence.
