## Claim Statement

Gravitational response is determined by the dynamics of Straton flow within the node network rather than solely by the instantaneous spatial distribution of mass.

## Assumptions

- A1. Mass corresponds to Straton content distributed across nodes.
- A2. Straton content evolves over time through node interactions and propagation constraints.
- A3. Stability-field gradients respond to the history and flow of Straton rather than to instantaneous configurations alone.
- A4. Temporal relaxation delays influence gravitational response.
- A5. Observable gravitational behavior emerges from aggregated Straton dynamics across regions.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence demonstrating gravitational response determined strictly by instantaneous mass distribution with no dependence on historical dynamics.
- Observations incompatible with any delay or memory effects in gravitational behavior.
- Demonstration that gravitational phenomena cannot depend on mass flow or redistribution processes.
- Empirical confirmation that gravity reacts instantaneously without any temporal modulation.
