## Claim Statement

The Straton quantity χ\chiχ contributes directly to the temporal relaxation delay τ\tauτ, such that systems with larger informational load experience longer response times in node-state evolution.

## Assumptions

- A1. Node updates require redistribution of informational load across the network.
- A2. The processing time associated with state transitions increases with Straton magnitude.
- A3. Temporal relaxation delay reflects finite propagation and processing constraints within the node network.
- A4. Relaxation dynamics depend on both informational load and network connectivity.
- A5. Macroscopic temporal effects emerge from aggregated node-level delays.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing relaxation times independent of Straton or mass-related quantities.
- Observations inconsistent with any monotonic relationship between informational load and temporal delay.
- Demonstration that temporal response in physical systems does not depend on mass or informational content.
- Empirical data requiring alternative mechanisms for relaxation unrelated to χ\chiχ.

## Evidence / Notes

- Conceptually consistent with inertia-related delays in classical and relativistic dynamics.
- Provides a mechanism linking informational load to observable temporal effects.
- Supports the Composite Time Model where delay contributes to observed time.
- Empirical validation depends on detection of measurable lag effects predicted by the theory.
