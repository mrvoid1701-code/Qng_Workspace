## Claim Statement

Stratonic lag in evolving mass–Straton distributions produces residual gravitational forces in dynamic systems, resulting from delayed adaptation of the stability field to changing configurations.

## Assumptions

- A1. Mass corresponds to Straton content distributed across the node network.
- A2. Gravitational response is mediated through the stability field Σ\SigmaΣ.
- A3. Stability-field evolution exhibits finite relaxation delay τ\tauτ.
- A4. Moving or accelerating systems generate spatial offsets between mass distribution and stability-field configuration.
- A5. Residual forces emerge from gradients associated with these delayed configurations.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing no residual gravitational forces in systems with changing mass configurations.
- Observations incompatible with any delay-induced gravitational effects.
- Demonstration that gravitational response is strictly instantaneous in dynamic systems.
- Empirical data contradicting predicted residual force magnitudes.

- Supports interpretation of certain anomalies as memory effects rather than new mass sources.
- Empirical validation depends on detection of measurable residual forces.
