## Claim Statement

Spatial structure is not a pre-existing background container but emerges from the relational connectivity and interaction topology among discrete informational nodes.

## Assumptions

- A1. Nodes possess no absolute embedding coordinates independent of their relations.
- A2. Physical adjacency is defined solely by relational connections within the node graph.
- A3. Geometric quantities (distance, curvature, dimensionality) arise from graph structure and node states.
- A4. Large-scale spatial continuity is an emergent approximation of dense relational networks.
- A5. Observable spatial measurements correspond to effective metrics derived from relational configurations.
