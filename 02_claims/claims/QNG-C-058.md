## Claim Statement

Gravitational lensing traces spatial gradients of the stability field Σ\SigmaΣ and may exhibit offsets relative to baryonic mass centers due to memory and lag dynamics in the stability-field evolution.

## Assumptions

- A1. Gravitational effects are mediated by the stability field Σ\SigmaΣ.
- A2. Lensing deflection depends on spatial variations of the effective gravitational field.
- A3. Stability-field memory retains contributions from past mass configurations.
- A4. Instantaneous baryonic mass distribution does not fully determine lensing behavior.
- A5. Observable offsets emerge from delayed stability-field adaptation.

## Potential Falsifier

- Observational evidence showing lensing always coincides precisely with baryonic mass centers.
- Measurements incompatible with any stability-field memory or lag effects.
- Demonstration that lensing behavior cannot deviate from instantaneous mass predictions.
- Empirical necessity for unseen mass to explain all observed offsets.
