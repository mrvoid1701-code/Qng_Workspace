## Claim Statement

The informational memory associated with each node is quantified by the Straton variable χ\chiχ, representing the latent informational load carried by matter within the node network.

## Assumptions

- A1. Nodes carry an informational quantity reflecting accumulated interaction history and inertia-like properties.
- A2. This informational quantity can be represented by a scalar measure χ\chiχ.
- A3. The Straton quantity is proportional to mass through the relation χ=m/c\chi = m/cχ=m/c.
- A4. Informational memory influences node dynamics, relaxation times, and stability behavior.
- A5. Macroscopic physical effects (e.g., inertia, gravitational lag) arise from collective distributions of χ\chiχ.

## Potential Falsifier

- Observations inconsistent with the proportionality relation χ=m/c\chi = m/cχ=m/c.
- Demonstration that node dynamics do not depend on informational load in any measurable way.
- Empirical necessity of additional independent variables beyond the Straton to explain physical behavior.
