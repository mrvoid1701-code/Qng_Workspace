## Claim Statement

Structures undergoing delayed gravitational collapse are predicted to exhibit spatial offsets between baryonic matter and effective dark-sector signatures, along with velocity mismatches arising from stability-field lag dynamics.

## Assumptions

- A1. Stability-field memory introduces delayed gravitational response relative to baryonic mass motion.
- A2. Rapid mass redistribution during collapse generates significant lag contributions.
- A3. Effective gravitational potential reflects both instantaneous and historical mass distributions.
- A4. Observable kinematic behavior depends on the evolving stability field rather than only current mass.
- A5. Offsets and velocity discrepancies emerge during non-equilibrium dynamical phases.
