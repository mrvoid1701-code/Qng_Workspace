## Claim Statement

Apparent superluminal correlations can arise from overlapping stability-memory fields between spatially separated systems, without requiring faster-than-light information transfer, due to shared historical dependencies in the network dynamics.

## Assumptions

- A1. Stability fields retain historical information through memory kernels.
- A2. Spatially separated regions may share correlated memory structures from prior interactions.
- A3. Observed correlations reflect common causal history rather than instantaneous transmission.
- A4. Information propagation remains constrained by causal update rules.
- A5. Observable quantum-like correlations emerge from underlying network dynamics.
