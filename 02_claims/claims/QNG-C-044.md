## Claim Statement

Inertia can be interpreted as an informational viscosity arising from resistance to changes in node configuration, scaling proportionally with the product of Straton content χ\chiχ and relaxation delay τ\tauτ.

## Assumptions

- A1. Informational load χ\chiχ represents resistance to state transitions in node dynamics.
- A2. Relaxation delay τ\tauτ characterizes the temporal response of node configurations.
- A3. Inertial behavior emerges from delayed adaptation to applied influences.
- A4. Macroscopic inertia reflects aggregated effects of informational resistance across node clusters.
- A5. Stability and persistence mechanisms contribute to inertial properties.

## Potential Falsifier

- Experimental evidence showing inertia independent of informational load or relaxation delay.
- Observations incompatible with any viscosity-like interpretation of inertia.
- Demonstration that inertial behavior cannot be represented through delayed response mechanisms.
- Empirical necessity for alternative mechanisms determining inertia.

## Evidence / Notes

- Provides a mechanistic interpretation of inertia as resistance arising from informational dynamics.
- Conceptually analogous to viscous response in complex systems.
- Links inertia to temporal and informational parameters within the model.
- Empirical validation depends on reproducing known inertial behavior quantitatively.
